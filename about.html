About Us

🌿 Our Story

It all began with motherhood.

Though I was born and brought up in a culturally rich family, both before and after marriage, I followed traditions with trust and devotion. Festivals were celebrated with joy, shlokas were chanted with love, and I believed I was passing on the same values effortlessly.

But motherhood changed everything.

My sons, with their innocent yet curious minds, began asking questions that stopped me in my tracks:

  • “Why should we take a bath before breakfast?”
  • “Why do we chant shlokas?”
  • “Why do we follow these rituals?”

I had always followed these practices… but now, for the first time, I had to explain them. And I didn’t have answers.

That’s when the journey truly began.

With a strong will to guide my children meaningfully, I started researching, learning, and rediscovering the depth behind our traditions. What began as a mother’s search for answers slowly turned into a deeper exploration of Sanatana Dharma, our Puranas, and the science behind our practices.

Then came COVID.

With more time at home, questions only grew—not just from my children, but from many parents. I realized something important:

  • Many of us follow traditions, but don’t fully understand why.
  • Even when we know, we struggle to explain them to our children.

At the same time, children today are growing in a fast-paced digital world:

  • Constant screen exposure
  • Instant gratification
  • Reduced patience and attention span

With working parents, limited time, and distance from traditional support systems, passing on values consciously has become a challenge.

This is not just my problem. This is the need of the hour.

And that’s how Samskaara Vriksham was born.

🌱 What We Stand For

Samskaara Vriksham was created with a simple yet powerful intention:

To help children live meaningfully, understand why we do what we do, and stay deeply connected to our roots, traditions, and Sanatana Dharma, while growing into confident, compassionate human beings.

💛 Our Purpose

We are not just teaching shlokas or stories.

We are nurturing:

  • Awareness

Because when a child understands their roots, they don’t just grow… they flourish—grounded, confident, and purposeful.
